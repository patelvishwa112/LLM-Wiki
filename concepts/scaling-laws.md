# Scaling Laws

Empirical relationships between language-model loss and compute, dataset
size, and parameter count. The original Kaplan et al. paper is among the
intellectual roots of Anthropic; subsequent Anthropic work has studied
which capabilities emerge predictably and which appear in sharp phase
transitions.

## What the sources say

- [[arxiv-2001.08361]] — "Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models": the
  founding paper
- [[arxiv-2202.07785]] — "Predictability and Surprise in LLMs": which
  capabilities follow scaling laws and which appear discontinuously
- [[arxiv-2303.03846]] — "Larger language models do in-context learning
  differently": ICL evolves qualitatively with scale

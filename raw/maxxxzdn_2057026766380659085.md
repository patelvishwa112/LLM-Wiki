## Content

Today we release Mosaic, a probabilistic weather model that shifts the Pareto frontier of ML weather forecasting.

It matches the skill of state-of-the-art models while generating a 24-member, 10-day global forecast in under 12 seconds on a single H100.

The forecasts that matter most are of extreme events such as frontal zones and tropical cyclones. Large weather models match or exceed physics-based forecasts at far lower cost, yet they degrade exactly at these fine scales.

Three failure modes traced: 1) deterministic training; 2) information bottleneck from compressive encoding; 3) predicting tendencies.

Mosaic solutions:
- Probabilistic model based on learned functional perturbations by @GoogleDeepMind. Instead of predicting the inherently smooth conditional mean of future states, it produces an ensemble whose members are each sharp, physically plausible realizations.
- Most models project weather onto a coarse latent mesh before any spatial mixing. When that mesh cannot represent fine-scale features, their energy turns into high-frequency artifacts. Mosaic avoids this by computing interactions at native resolution, before coarsening.

# Units of Time

Alfred Lin's son, finishing his freshman year of high school, asked how to think about time. Lin's answer: three critical units.

**The moment.** "The days are long, but the years are short." Every second counts — you never get that moment back. Make every moment count. Don't let time pass you by.

**The chapter.** More nebulous — could be a day, a year, or a decade. Looking backward, what period marks a significant milestone? Hopefully you feel accomplishment. At minimum, you carry what you've learned into the next one.

**The book.** At the end of your life, will your book tell the story of someone who fought hard on a worthwhile journey and left an enduring impact? Was it a purpose-filled, meaningful story? Who will line up to tell your story?

## The New Revelation

Today's builders must accomplish orders of magnitude more than previous generations in the same time. Fifteen years ago, the most valuable companies were $300-400B. Today, several are $4-5T. The distance from seed to legendary has grown 10x. Human lifespan hasn't moved. We have roughly the same moments, chapters, and pages as our predecessors — but must cover 10x the distance in that same fixed time.

If the game has gotten harder, the question is not how to work more hours. It's which moments, chapters, and books are worth writing at all. Every choice to work on one thing is a choice not to work on something else. The opportunity cost of attention has never been higher.

The founders Lin admires most understand this intuitively. They find the one or two questions whose answers can change paradigms. They know 1% better every day creates a different person and company by year's end. They've signed up for the long game and will compound for decades.

> "Your time is limited, so don't waste it living someone else's life. Don't be trapped by dogma, which is living with the results of other people's thinking. Don't let the noise of others' opinions drown out your own inner voice. And most important, have the courage to follow your heart and intuition." — Steve Jobs

The moment is finite. The chapter is your choice. The book is your legacy.

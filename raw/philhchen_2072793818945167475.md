# Career skills in the agent-native era

**Author:** Phil Chen (@philhchen)  
**Posted:** 2026-07-02  
**Engagement:** 1,070 likes, 97 reposts

AI models get better at anything you can write a loss function for, and school is mostly loss functions: well-defined problems graded against known answers. Therefore, the valuable work of the next decade is everything that can't be graded within the span of model training.

During my 6 years of work, I've been fortunate to have collaborated with amazing people from companies of all sizes, from my own startup, to Helm AI (15→50 FTE), Scale AI (500→1500 FTE), OpenAI (1500→3000 FTE), and Google (100,000+ FTE). As a founder, I spend a lot of time thinking about the right hires for our company's present and future. Because we are fully agent-native, our needs are very different from any company where I've previously worked.

## Six themes

**1. Focus on resources that are truly limited** — Capital is abundant; time, relationships, and reputation are not. Do meaningful work visible to reputable peers; avoid vibe-coding quick bucks without real value.

**2. Learn to find problems in addition to solving them** — In agent-native hiring, LeetCode/system design correlate weakly. Interviews target: understand environment, pick problems worth solving, execute under constraints. Problem selection + token/time allocation matter; candidates differ in how efficiently they collaborate with agents using intuition and context.

**3. Work on the most ambitious form of a problem** — Bitter-lesson framing for careers: power-law outcomes accelerated; durable value needs extreme focus on ambitious problems. Pick companies on most ambitious formulation of their problem and realistic shot; pick roles on frontier access.

**4. Sprint the last mile** — Alfred Lin: last 10% is 90% of work and reward. Median agent output is sloppy-prompt quality; value from unique perspective and polish. Iterate; sometimes restart with next-gen models. Practice polish, architecture, scalability, creativity on projects.

**5. Increase both xG and efficiency** — Soccer analogy: expected opportunities vs conversion. Personal anecdotes turning down Anthropic/Cursor for DeepMind/OpenAI aligned to interests. Humans still select meaningful problems and allocate capital. Early-stage bets: team + market over current product (Anthropic's early Slackbot example).

**6. You can break into research now** — Use models, build evals/intuitions; public leaderboards (e.g. Keller Jordan); academic credits (Modal). Researcher mindset = curiosity, infra fights, system-level debugging, articulating value for compute—possible outside frontier labs.

## Closing

Focus on finding interesting problems and delivering extraordinary results.

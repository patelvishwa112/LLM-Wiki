## Post content

Amit Shekhar (@amitiitbhu) wrote a detailed technical breakdown of **Sakana Fugu** — a family of orchestrator models from Sakana AI (Tokyo lab; co-founded by a co-author of the original Transformer paper).

Instead of answering questions directly, **Fugu acts like a conductor**: it reads the query, decides which frontier models (GPT-5.5, Claude Opus 4.8, Gemini 3.1, etc.) should work on it, how they should collaborate, and how to synthesize their outputs into one final answer.

### Two variants

| Variant | Goal | Approach | Speed vs quality |
|---------|------|----------|------------------|
| **Fugu** | Everyday use | Picks **one best model** per query using a lightweight selection head | Fast (~single-model latency) |
| **Fugu-Ultra** | Hardest problems | Builds full **agentic workflows** (multiple models, subtasks, access controls) | Slower, higher quality |

### Core ideas

- **Why needed**: No single frontier model is best at everything (GPT strong at math/planning, Claude at software engineering/debugging, Gemini at science/implementation). Fugu dynamically combines strengths.
- **Collective intelligence**: Orchestration as a scaling law — make models work together intelligently instead of only scaling size.
- **Fugu training**: Small selection head on hidden state scores models without generating text (fast). Two stages: supervised fine-tuning with soft probability targets (KL divergence), then evolutionary strategies (sep-CMA-ES) on end-to-end tasks.
- **Fugu-Ultra training**: Generates workflows (subtasks + model assignment + visibility). Trained with **GRPO** (Group Relative Policy Optimization). Mitigates orchestration collapse via intra-workflow isolation + persistent shared memory across conversations.
- **Performance**: State-of-the-art results; Fugu-Ultra often beats pool models (e.g. 73.7 SWE-Bench Pro vs Claude 69.2). Strong on novel tasks: Rubik's cube, blindfold chess, simulated stock trading, mechanical CAD.

### Emergent strategies

1. **Debate & aggregation** — Multiple models solve independently; aggregator combines best parts.
2. **Build & debug** — GPT builds; Claude joins for debugging/security at the right moments.
3. **Specialist routing** — Best model per subtask (math vs security vs facts).

## Post

> Most people lose their best ideas by noon.
> Save this :)
> This setup captures everything, connects the dots automatically, and hands you a synthesized briefing every morning.. for next to nothing.
> What You'll Need
> Four tools. Each has a specific role. None of them overlap.
> Obsidian — local vault, plain markdown files, you own everything
> Vellum — intelligence layer that reads, connects, and synthesizes your notes. Credits at cost.
> Readwise — aggregates every highlight across articles, Kindle, Twitter bookmarks, Pocket into Obsidian
> N8N — open source automation, connects capture sources to vault
> Step 1: Design the Vault Architecture
> Open Obsidian, create a new vault, name it CHIEF. Then build this exact folder structure:
>
> Every folder has a single purpose. Here's the logic behind each one:
> 00-INBOX is raw landing ground. Nothing gets organized at capture time — speed matters more than structure in the moment.
> 01-CAPTURES is sorted by note type, not topic. This is the most consequential architectural decision in the entire system. When you organize by topic, a crypto insight and a behavioral psychology observation never share a folder. When you organize by type, they both land in patterns/ — and Vellum finds the bridge between them.
> 02-CONNECTIONS is where synthesized thinking lives. These aren't cleaned-up captures. They're emergent insights — new ideas that only exist because two unrelated notes were finally in the same room.
> 03-PROJECTS holds everything related to what you're currently building or researching. One subfolder per active project.
> 04-VELLUM is the intelligence layer's working directory. This is where Vellum reads its instructions and stores its output files.
> Step 2: Install Three Plugins
> Templater — Runs dynamic templates with actual logic. Auto-format notes when N8N pushes captures into the vault.
> Dataview — Turns your vault into a queryable database. Filter notes by tag, date, or any property. Essential for weekly connection sessions.
> Obsidian Git — Backs up entire vault to a private GitHub repo every hour.
> Step 3: Automate Everything at the Capture Layer
> Articles & Highlights: Set up Readwise, install browser extension. Highlight lines that matter. Readwise stores automatically. Enable native Obsidian sync.
> Quick Capture From Anywhere: Build a Telegram bot that accepts any message and drops it directly into 00-INBOX via N8N workflow. ~30 minutes to set up, runs indefinitely.
> Voice Notes: Record voice memo, run through Whisper for transcript, paste into Telegram bot → hits INBOX automatically.
> Step 4: Write the VELLUM.md File
> Most important file in the system. Without it, every Vellum session starts cold. With it, Vellum behaves like a collaborator who has been reading your notes for months.
> Create at 04-VELLUM/VELLUM.md and fill it out completely.
> Step 5: Connect Vellum to Your Vault
> Create account at vellum.ai. Connect to Obsidian vault folder. Run test prompt: "Read my VELLUM.md file and tell me in one paragraph what you understand about this vault, who I am, and what your role is inside this system."
> If generic, VELLUM.md needs more detail.
> Step 6: Build the Four Core Workflows
> Save each as markdown file inside 04-VELLUM/workflows/.
> Workflow 1 — Process Inbox: Read every note from 00-INBOX (last 24h). Determine which 01-CAPTURES subfolder, sharpen into one punchy sentence, move it. Report: total handled, where each went, patterns noticed, one connection worth exploring.
> Workflow 2 — Daily Brief: Schedule via N8N at 6am. Read INBOX (24h) + CAPTURES (7 days). Find 3 most interesting links with older notes, identify one pattern across the week's reading, give one question worth sitting with. Save as brief-{{date}}.md in INBOX.
> Workflow 3 — Weekly Connections: Every Sunday. Read all CAPTURES from last 7 days across all subfolders. Surface 3-5 strong connections: Type A (same principle across domains), Type B (contradiction), Type C (pattern across 3+ notes), Type D (question one note answers from another). Create notes in 02-CONNECTIONS.
> Workflow 4 — Deep Research Session: "Read everything in my vault related to [topic]. Tell me: what do I actually believe based on my notes? What have I saved that contradicts that? What perspective is missing? What's the single most important question I haven't asked yet?"
> Step 7: The Daily Ritual — 15 Minutes
> Min 1-5: Send raw captures to Telegram bot. Observed patterns, questions, numbers. Raw. Unpolished. Just get it in.
> Min 6-10: Run Process Inbox workflow. Read the report.
> Min 11-15: Read the daily brief generated at 6am. Synthesis of a full week's inputs with connections surfaced automatically — before opening any social platform.
> Step 8: The Weekly Connection Session
> Every Sunday, run Weekly Connections across full week of captures. Review connection notes in 02-CONNECTIONS. Pick two strongest — those are your best ideas for the week.
> Step 9: Close the Loop
> Whenever a thesis plays out, a pattern proves correct, or a connection leads somewhere real — open the relevant note and record what happened. Vellum reads these updates. Over months it stops just knowing what you think and starts understanding what thinking has been right for you.
> Monthly synthesis: "Read everything added in last 30 days. Tell me: what beliefs am I forming that I haven't stated yet? What pattern keeps appearing across domains? What is the single highest-leverage thing I could be thinking about? What am I clearly not reading that gaps in my notes suggest?"
> What Happens Over Time
> 30 days: Vault feels genuinely useful. Daily brief surfaces forgotten notes exactly relevant to current work.
> 90 days: Vellum connects month-one notes to month-three captures. Vault knows things about your thinking you don't consciously remember.
> 6 months: Complete record of every belief held and changed. Every pattern that appeared before you recognized it. Every connection → insight → decision → outcome. Intelligence compounding while you lived your life.
> Most people start from zero every single day.
> This system makes sure you never have to.

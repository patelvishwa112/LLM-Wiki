# Knowledge Distillation (KD) — One of the Most Important Techniques for Smaller, Capable Models

**Post by @TheTuringPost (June 20, 2026)**

**Knowledge Distillation (KD)** — one of the most important and practical techniques for building smaller, capable models.

### Core Idea
Instead of training a small model from scratch, you train a large “teacher” model first, then transfer its knowledge to a smaller “student” model. The student doesn’t just learn the correct answers (hard labels); it learns the *teacher’s confidence distribution* across all possible answers. This “dark knowledge” — how the teacher ranks wrong answers and sees similarities between classes — is surprisingly valuable.

### The Softmax + Temperature Mechanism
The key trick is using **softmax with temperature (T)**:
- T = 1 → standard “hard” probabilities (one class gets almost all the mass)
- T > 1 → “soft” probabilities that spread probability mass more evenly, revealing the teacher’s nuanced beliefs

The student is trained to match these soft targets (usually with KL divergence) while also sometimes matching the true labels. Hinton, Vinyals, and Dean popularized the term in their 2015 paper, building on earlier model compression work from 2006.

### Three Main Types of Distillation
1. **Response-based** — Match the teacher’s final output probabilities (the classic method).
2. **Feature-based** — Make the student’s intermediate layer activations match the teacher’s (e.g. FitNets). The student learns the teacher’s *internal representations*, not just the answers.
3. **Relation-based** — Transfer relationships between different samples or between layers (more advanced).

### Training Schemes
- **Offline**: Train teacher completely, then use it to distill the student.
- **Online**: Teacher and student are trained together (often mutually).
- **Self-distillation**: The model learns from itself (e.g. its own deeper version or earlier checkpoints).

The post also covers recent momentum from DeepSeek’s successful distillation of DeepSeek-R1, scaling laws for distillation, advanced algorithms that have emerged over the years, and the benefits/limitations of the approach.

# Effort is Linear, Position is Exponential (and 4 Other Principles)

**@incentivising** posted a long-form essay (June 20, 2026) addressing a recurring DM: "even though I put in a ton of effort, it seems like I never get anywhere."

The core message: **You are not deficient. You lack knowledge of mechanisms.** The old "work hard and you'll be rewarded" model has broken down. Success now depends on understanding hidden principles that institutions don't teach.

## The 5 Principles

1. **Effort is Linear, Position is Exponential (The Types of Leverage)**  
   Raw effort scales linearly. Position and leverage scale exponentially. Most people optimize the wrong variable.

2. **Invisible Competence Is Just a Hobby (The Costly Signal)**  
   True competence must be visible and costly to fake (Spence 1973 signaling). Private skill without external validation remains a hobby.

3. **The Market Is Indifferent to Effort (The Misconception)**  
   Markets reward outcomes and position, not hours invested. Effort without the right problem or leverage is invisible.

4. **Most People Are Solving The Wrong Problem (The Harsh Diagnosis)**  
   Enormous effort is wasted on problems that don't compound or that the market doesn't value.

5. **Time Spent is a Cost. Results Are the Return (The Institutional Lie)**  
   Modern institutions still reward inputs (time, credentials) over outputs. The game has changed; the scorecard hasn't been updated for most people.

The post draws on Rosen (1981) superstar economics, Spence signaling theory, and Ericsson deliberate practice research. It argues the real divide today is not income but "an understanding of the world itself."

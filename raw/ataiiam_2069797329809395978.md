## Visible post text (reconstructed from x_search)

The self-learning agent everyone is excited about is not what most products actually need. The highest-value signal is almost completely ignored: **real user interactions** with your product.

Atai breaks down self-learning into **three distinct layers** (inspired by Harrison Chase's framing):

### 1. The Model (The Weights)

- What people usually imagine when they hear "self-learning."
- Approaches: Karpathy's AutoResearch, MIT SEAL, DeepMind's AlphaEvolve.
- **Reality check:** Only works when you have a free, automatic scorer (math, code, benchmarks). Useless for most real-world domains like support or sales where there's no objective auto-grader.
- Mostly stays in research labs.

### 2. The Harness (The code, loops, tools, prompts, verification layers around the model)

- Methods range from hand-built loops → agents proposing changes from traces → fully autonomous self-rewriting harnesses → prebuilt stacks (Microsoft).
- LangChain's Deep Agents and "Self-Harness" approaches showed big gains on benchmarks by improving the harness while keeping model weights frozen.
- Still limited: many approaches require human approval or free scoring.

### 3. The Context (Memory & skills stored as readable text)

- Where most practical progress happens.
- Includes semantic (facts), episodic (past experiences), and procedural (how to handle situations) memory.
- Approaches: Letta/OpenClaw (background memory rewriting), Hermes (skillbook from failures), Anthropic/Manus (saving good runs as reusable SKILL.md files).
- Advantage: Human-readable, portable across models, and easy to edit/rollback.

### The key insight

All the methods above mostly learn from the *agent's own runs*.

The best signal is **what your users do** when they work alongside the agent (especially when they override or correct it). This is the "unfakable" signal — a real human making the right call.

**CopilotKit** and the open **AG-UI** protocol capture the full interaction between user and agent in real time and turn those moments into procedural memory for future runs.

Call to action: reach out for agents that improve from real usage (self-hostable in your own infrastructure).

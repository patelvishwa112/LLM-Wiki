# Token Capital Is Not Capital (Without Scaffolding)

Satya Nadella dropped a post on Saturday that got 60 million views.

He introduced a concept he calls "token capital."  
His argument: every company needs to build token capital alongside human capital.

He's right about the direction. He skipped the part that makes it work.

Nadella described companies building AI systems that compound over time. He called it a "hill climbing machine."

He didn't talk about what happens when you try to build that machine inside a company that can't draw its own workflow map.

I run an AI engineering firm. We embed engineers inside client teams. We've seen this exact problem play out across dozens of engagements.

This article covers four things:
- Why token capital is not capital by itself
- The equation that determines whether your AI compounds or produces slop
- Seven layers between your process foundation and business value
- Four signals from this week that prove the same point

### Token Capital Is Not Capital

Token capital becomes capital when you embed it in a system that converts human judgment into reusable workflows. The scaffolding around the model is the asset. The model by itself is a suggestion engine.

Building those loops takes process design. Process is the boring thing your COO brings up while everyone else wants to move fast. That process needs governance. Another word nobody wants to hear.

Without process and governance, you get AI slop. Most companies are shipping AI slop right now.

We see it constantly. A company buys the best model. Points it at their workflows. Gets output. That output works. But it doesn't compound. No feedback loop. No governance layer defining what "good" looks like. No process architecture the model can learn from. The model runs on vibes. Fast vibes, but vibes.

### The Multiplication Equation

AI value is a product, not a sum.

**AI Value = Human Capital × Scaffolding × Feedback Loops × Model**

Multiplication. If any multiplier is zero, the whole thing is zero.

Nadella said human capital doesn't become *less* valuable as token capital grows. He's right. But he undersold it. Human capital is a multiplier. Remove it and the entire thing collapses.

Your people become *more* important in an AI-driven company, not less.

We've seen mid-tier models with proper scaffolding outperform frontier models running without feedback loops. Repeatedly. The disciplined team beats the expensive model. Every time.

### Seven Layers Most Companies Skip

Most companies start at the model (Layer 7) and skip the foundation.

The seven layers are:
1. Process design
2. Governance
3. Knowledge architecture
4. Human judgment
5. Feedback loops
6. Scaffolding
7. Model

Bottom three = foundation.  
Middle three = compounding loop.  
Model = thin layer on top.

Data from Info-Tech's "Future of IT 2026" survey highlights the gap: 58% of organizations have AI in enterprise strategies (up from 26% last year), but only 30% feel prepared to operationalize it.

### Four Signals From This Week

1. **Nadella's post** — Came shortly after Microsoft launched in-house MAI models and restructured its OpenAI deal. He's signaling that organizational capability is the permanent advantage.
2. **Apple at WWDC** — Made models interchangeable in Siri (iOS 27) and Xcode 27. You can swap between ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. If the model is swappable, it's not your moat.
3. **Enterprise readiness gap** — The 28-point gap between strategy and operationalization is exactly the skipped layers (process, governance, feedback).
4. **US government shutdown of Anthropic's Fable 5** — Pulled just three days after launch due to jailbreak vulnerabilities. Even the most capable model failed because the governance/scaffolding layer was missing.

**Core thesis:** The model is commoditizing fast. The scaffolding, process, governance, and human judgment around it are what create durable value. Companies that skip the foundation will keep producing expensive AI slop.

### The One Question Test

Before building your next agentic workflow, ask:  
**Can you draw the process map the agent will operate inside?**

If no → you have a scaffolding problem. No model fixes that.

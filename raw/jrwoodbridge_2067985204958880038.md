# Post-Agent Companies

**Justin Woodbridge (@jrwoodbridge)**, founder of Phoebe (in-home healthcare agents/network), co-authored with Yoni Rechtman (@yrechtman).

## Core Thesis

We are approaching the endgame of the agent wave. The new fundamental primitive is **infinite, free, on-demand cognitive labor** (via ever-cheaper, open-source, local models).

This resets everything:

- Past paradigms (PC, web, cloud, mobile) each created new assumptions for how to build companies.
- Current AI companies mostly fall into two buckets:
  - "System of action" vertical SaaS (bolt AI onto traditional software thinking).
  - "Neofirms"/AI rollups (use agents to do the work and capture the margin in traditional businesses).

Both miss the endgame.

## The Post-Agent Company

When intelligence becomes a commodity (approaching free), the scarce resources shift to **context, attention, trust, brand, and network effects**.

Post-agent companies:

- Give away the labor (digital workers doing the actual work for free or near-free) as a GTM and flywheel strategy.
- Position themselves as **asset-light attention aggregators, coordination infrastructure, and trusted clearinghouses/agent-native marketplaces**.
- Monetize through transactions, advertising, attention routing, or other network-driven models rather than software licenses or service margins.
- Use the commodity labor to rapidly gather proprietary context and bootstrap network effects that competitors (still trying to charge for software or services) cannot easily match.
- Run the *entire company* on internal agents — every function becomes engineering-assisted. Teams are deeply technical *and* entrepreneurial at all levels. Headcount thinking is replaced by spinning up on-demand agent pods.

He explicitly contrasts this with "neofirms" that try to control and monetize the work directly, arguing you can't simultaneously maximize network effects and control in messy physical verticals.

## Phoebe as the Example

Woodbridge and Rechtman are applying this at **Phoebe** (Recurrence) in the massive, fragmented in-home healthcare market:

- Agents for agencies (scheduling/operations)
- Talent agents for caregivers (better shifts/pay)
- Care advisors for families

They treat the *network of agents* as the real product. The company itself runs on a central "Phoebe Core" with heavy internal agent usage across CX, growth, engineering (self-healing product), etc. They emphasize hiring AI-native, "AGI-pilled," commercially minded people who love the game over traditional credentials.

# Verifiability constraint — RLVR and unverifiable domains

**Author:** Tanay Jaipuria (@tanayj)  
**Posted:** 2026-07-02  
**Engagement:** 274 likes, 28 reposts

Framed from Dario Amodei (Dwarkesh podcast): ~90% confident in "country of geniuses in a data center" within 10 years; residual uncertainty = **tasks you can't verify** (Mars mission planning, fundamental discovery like CRISPR, novels). Coding likely 1–2 years except irreducible uncertainty.

## I. The verifiability constraint

**RLVR** (RL with verifiable rewards): checkable answers → reinforce good reasoning paths. Math/code fit: clean cheap rewards at scale; SWE-bench progress; IMO gold-level 2025.

**Jason Wei "verifier's law":** ease of training AI ∝ how verifiable the task is.

Most valuable work lacks easy checkers (memos, design, building businesses with long-horizon real-world feedback).

RLHF / Constitutional AI = reward when no checker—but capability jumps in subjective domains lag RLVR; engagement optimization concern.

## II. Techniques

- **Rubrics as rewards** — instance-specific checklists; LLM judge per item (Scale AI; +31% relative on HealthBench vs plain judge); OpenRubrics at scale
- **Generative reward models** — reason then score
- **Process reward models** — grade intermediate steps for long-horizon tasks

Approximate checkers via decomposed rubrics on outputs or process.

## III. Companies

1. **Sell verifier + data to labs** — Mercor, Surge, Micro1; rubric items programmatically checkable; @taste_ai_ for subjective design/taste (RLHF averages away taste)
2. **Formalize the domain** — Lean proofs self-check; Pramaana Labs for tax/law/healthcare formal verification
3. **Own the whole loop** — physical world as reward: Periodic Labs (materials), Isomorphic Labs (drugs), Lila Sciences (autonomous labs)

## Closing

Next leap = bringing RLVR-style gains to harder-to-verify economy; open whether RLVR generalizes or new breakthrough needed.

# Iceberg: The Prompts That Unleash Opus 4.8

Opus 4.8 dropped last week. Most people switched the model, typed their usual prompt, and waited for it to feel smarter. It didn't. They were handed a Ferrari and kept driving it in first gear.

A new model isn't something you receive. It's something you learn.

## Layer 1: Effort Control (The Surface)

New setting: Low → Medium → High → Extra → Max. Controls how hard Claude thinks before answering. Leave Adaptive Thinking on.

- Low/Medium: quick questions, 80% of what you do
- High: normal work
- Max: the decision that matters. Reserve it.

Example: Max caught what Low missed — reframed the task, flagged a real trap, found the only metric that matters. Same model, same prompt. The difference was depth, not length.

## Layer 2: Negative Space (Below the Surface)

Tell Claude what to LEAVE OUT. 40% of most answers is padding. Fix with:

- "Skip the obvious — assume I already know the basics."
- "No hedging. Take a position."
- "Don't list options. Give me the one you'd pick and why."
- "Cut the disclaimers."

Before: five paragraphs of "it depends." After: real numbers, one split, flags its own weakest points. The first answer protects Claude. The second helps you.

## Layer 3: The Wrong Question (Going Deeper)

Before Claude answers — make it challenge your premise:

- "Before you answer — what assumption am I making that could be wrong?"
- "Is this the right problem to solve, or a symptom of a deeper one?"
- "Steelman the case that I'm asking the wrong question entirely."
- "What would someone smarter than me ask instead?"

Example: "Pay off my 6% car loan faster" → reframed to: do you have higher-interest debt? Are you missing a 401(k) match? It became a capital-allocation problem, not a payoff plan.

## Layer 4: The Second Pass (Deeper Still)

4.8 is the first version built to catch its own errors — 4x less likely to let a flaw slip through. But it won't run that check unless you send it back:

- "Now audit your own answer. Where is it most likely wrong?"
- "Find the weakest claim you just made and stress-test it."
- "Review this like a senior engineer reviewing a junior's PR. Be ruthless."
- "Flag everything you're not fully sure about."

## Layer 5: The Persona (The Edge of the Map)

The model doesn't have one level of skill — it has thousands, layered by domain. Which one you get depends entirely on who you tell it to be.

- "You're a CFO reviewing this with your own capital on the line."
- "Answer as a forensic accountant whose entire job is finding what's being hidden."
- "You're a short-seller building the bear case. Where does this fall apart?"
- "Respond as someone who's done this 500 times and lost money twice."

4.8 is the first version honest enough to hold an expert's standards for real — including telling you no. Older models were just confident cosplay. The persona gives the lens. The new model gives it the spine.

## The Only Variable Is You

Opus 4.8 didn't ship to be smarter on its own. It shipped to be lethal in the right hands — honest enough to take a side, ruthless enough to audit itself, deep enough to become any expert you name. None of it turns on by default. All of it turns on with a prompt.

The model is identical for everyone who opens it. The only variable is you.

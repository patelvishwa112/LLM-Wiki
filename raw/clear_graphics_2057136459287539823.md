go to any yc batch page and click through 20 startup websites. 
16 of them will be using one of four fonts: 
- inter
- geist
- söhne
- pp neue montreal
this is a convergence of access, optimization, and herd behavior that created the most homogeneous era of startup typography in the HISTORY of tech
this is what each font signals, why they all got adopted, and why using something different is actually a competitive advantage right now...
inter: the default:
inter font
inter was designed by rasmus andersson specifically for computer screens. 
open source, free, available on google font, ships as a system font on most modern devices, and loads instantly because it's often already cached in the browser
inter became the startup DEFAULT because it's genuinely good at everything and costs nothing. 
its clean at small sizes, readable in dense data tables, professional enough for enterprise, friendly enough for consumer
basically the typographic equivalent of a white t-shirt lol
it signals: "we're a technology company and we didn't overthink the font choice." 
and communicates competence and professionalism without any specific personality. 
inter is the absence of a typographic opinion
who uses it: roughly 40% of startup websites. 
it is the single most popular font in saas so if you use inter, you look like a startup. 
which is fine if looking like every other startup is acceptable to you
geist: the vercel aesthetic:
geist font
geist was created by vercel and released in late 2023. 
feels like inter's slightly more refined cousin (rounder curves, friendlier letter shapes, a touch more modern) 
free for commercial use and installable via npm which means it integrates seamlessly into react and next.js projects
geist became popular because it shipped with vercel's ecosystem. 
every next.js template, every vercel deployment guide, every shadcn/ui component defaults to geist. 
the vercel stack is the MOST popular frontend stack in startups right now so geist inherited that distribution automatically
what it signals: "we use the modern stack"
geist communicates technical sophistication and alignment with the vercel/next.js ecosystem. 
it kind of says "our engineering team knows what they're doing" in a way that inter doesn't because geist is associated specifically with cutting- dge frontend development
who uses it: roughly 15-20% of startup websites and growing fast. especially prevalent in developer tools, ai products, and anything built on next.js
söhne: the premium signal:
söhne font
söhne is designed by kris sowersby at klim type foundry in new zealand. 
it captures the memory of akzidenz grotesk filtered through helvetica's influence
specifically, it ENVOKES the analogue feel of standard medium used in new york city subway signage

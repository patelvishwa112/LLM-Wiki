# How to make AI agents behave like actual employees

**darkzodchi (@zodchiii)** — Jul 2, 2026

Framing: models are already capable; bottleneck is **work environment** (waiting, escalation, persistence, multi-session goals)—not raw intelligence.

Industry context: Skills, AgentKit, background agents—race to turn prompt-answerers into outcome owners.

## Six shifts

### 1. Wait properly
Not frozen chat spinners. Explicit states:
- Question cards with choices when input needed
- Queued tasks visible on timeline
- Connection cards for missing OAuth (Gmail, Slack)
- Stop for risky approval

### 2. Ask before guessing
Structured unblock questions (option, source, conflict handling, proceed without context?) as cards—not buried in prose.

### 3. Sign-off on material actions
Emails/posts, secrets/API keys, real-world record edits, new service connections → approval gate pattern.

### 4. Coordinate as a team
Coordinator routing; task board with parent/child tasks; isolated threads; state survives reload—not opaque parallel bots.

### 5. Own outcomes over weeks
Scheduled recurring work (weekly report, ongoing research monitor, pipeline tracking)—**responsibilities** not one-off tasks. Mentions cron/VPS DIY vs hosted platform.

### 6. (Implicit in “all six”) Environment that survives ops
Session recovery, work contracts, 24/7 infra—article argues hand-rolled VPS stacks fail on reboot; promotes Teamly as packaged implementation.

## DIY note (author)

Subagents, cron on VPS, webhooks for approvals, hand-rolled persistence—works until infra flakes.

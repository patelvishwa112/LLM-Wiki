# How Jim Simon's RL Engine Cuts Execution Costs (DQN Framework)

**@RitOnchain (Venus)** — X Article, Jun 2026. Full text via Playwright.

*Headline references 47%; body cites 23% implementation shortfall vs TWAP over three months at a systematic fund.*

The execution desk at a major systematic fund had a problem. Their TWAP algorithm was predictable - predatory HFT firms could front-run it. Their VWAP algorithm was better but couldn't adapt to real-time liquidity shocks. They needed something that learned.

In late 2024, they deployed a DQN-based execution agent. Within three months, implementation shortfall dropped 23% versus TWAP. The agent wasn't just executing - it was adapting to order book dynamics in real-time.

This is the exact architecture, with code.

## Why Static Execution Algorithms Fail

**TWAP** splits a large order equally across time periods — simple, predictable, exploitable.

**VWAP** sizes slices proportional to expected volume — still a pre-computed schedule that can't react to sudden liquidity drying, adverse mid-execution price moves, or information leakage from your own pattern.

RL treats execution as a **sequential decision problem**: observe state each step, decide how much to trade now vs later.

## MDP formulation

- **State**: remaining inventory, time left, LOB imbalance (top 5), price divergence from arrival price
- **Actions**: discrete trade buckets (0, Q_min … 4Q_min) or continuous fraction of remaining inventory
- **Reward**: market impact penalty, unexecuted inventory penalty (urgency increases with time), aggression penalty
- **Dynamics**: unknown, non-stationary → model-free RL

## Code (PyTorch DQN)

Includes: `DQNExecutionAgent`, `ReplayBuffer`, `ExecutionTrainer` (Double DQN, ε-greedy, target net updates), `execution_reward()` with terminal penalty for leftover inventory, `ExecutionEnvironment` (synthetic LOB; production → LOBSTER/ITCH/ABIDES), `train_execution_agent()` loop.

## Production

1. Expand state (depth profile, trade flow, vol regime, correlated assets)
2. Continuous actions → DDPG/PPO
3. Historical replay validation (ABIDES, LOBSTER)
4. Hard risk limits (max participation ~20% volume, max order size)
5. Multi-objective (cost + TWAP tracking error + completion certainty)

## Closing

Execution optimization ≈ 5–15 bps/trade. Won't fix a bad signal. **Build the signal first. Then optimize execution.**

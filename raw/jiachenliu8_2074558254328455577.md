# How to Distill a $10M+ AI Researcher

**Author:** Amber Liu (@JIACHENLIU8)  
**Posted:** Jul 7, 2026  
**TL;DR:** Research Process > Research Outcomes

## Thesis

Top researcher "intuition" = navigating uncertainty — which questions matter, artifact vs insight, which experiments resolve doubt, which directions waste months. Much of this is **compressed empirical knowledge** (failed runs, leakage patterns, scale collapse, variable traps), i.e. **process knowledge**, not outcome knowledge.

Papers communicate results; infrastructure treats **process traces** (failed experiments, aborted hypotheses, pivot reasons) as waste — discarding the highest-value data.

## Research World Model

Externalize exploration in a queryable archive instead of implicit session context: explore → hypothesize → act → observe → reflect.

**ARC-AGI-3 (ls20) experiment:** Claude Code + Opus 4.8, ~1% frontier pass rate.
- Without world model: 5,000+ aimless steps, context-bound.
- With evolving cross-context archive (laws, recipes, failure states): mapped mechanics, referenced history, **63-step** final level clear.

Research as living knowledge graph → cross-context retrieval, compounding lessons, scaled uncertainty navigation.

Distilling elite researchers = **architectural innovation** — executable research artifacts that iterate understanding; shift from answer-generator to **persistent research system** / OS for trustworthy AI science.

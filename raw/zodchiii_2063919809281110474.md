# How to build a 4-agent trading desk that finds and trades opportunities while you sleep

**Author:** @zodchiii (darkzodchi)
**Source:** https://x.com/zodchiii/status/2063919809281110474
**Date:** 2026-06-08

---

Four AI agents can run your trades while you sleep.

Most people just open the app, see a tweet about some coin, ape in, refresh the chart all day, and bail when it dumps 10%. Real setup looks different.

4 agents - each doing one job. First one digs up ideas. Second one stress-tests them. Third one actually places the trade. Fourth one babysits the position once it's live.

## The 4-agent setup

- **Hunter** — scans markets, news, sentiment, finds potential setups
- **Analyst** — validates each setup with backtests and risk math
- **Executor** — places the trade through your broker
- **Monitor** — watches the position and exits when rules say so

Skip any of them and the system breaks.

Horizon (@horizon_trade_x) is the infrastructure that lets agents 2 and 3 work without writing a single line of Python.

## Agent 1: Hunter

Scans for setups around the clock. Inputs: News feeds (Reuters, Bloomberg API, earnings calendars), Social sentiment (X, Reddit r/wallstreetbets, StockTwits), Technical scanners (RSI extremes, breakouts, gap-ups), Macro events (Fed dates, CPI prints, NFP releases).

Output: 5-15 potential setups per day with short descriptions. Built as a Claude agent on a 15-minute cron job, pulling from 3-5 data sources, scoring against watchlist criteria. Cost: ~$30/month in Claude API calls.

The Hunter doesn't trade. It only reports. That separation matters.

## Agent 2: Analyst

Takes Hunter's shortlist and decides what's actually tradeable. Checks: backtest performance on 5+ years of historical data, Sharpe ratio, max drawdown, win rate vs profit factor, correlation with existing positions, risk/reward at current price (1:2 minimum).

Horizon turns plain English strategy descriptions into real rules, tests on 5 years of past data, and shows if it actually made money. Without a tool like this, you'd be writing code by hand and waiting hours.

## Agent 3: Executor

Handles: how to buy (immediate vs limit), how much to risk (max 1% per trade), where to buy (Alpaca for US stocks, Binance for crypto, Kraken/Coinbase for backup, E*TRADE for options).

The Executor doesn't think. It just does what the Analyst already approved. Horizon connects directly to Alpaca, Binance, Kraken, Coinbase, and E*TRADE.

## Agent 4: Monitor

Watches everything post-trade: tracks P&L in real time (Telegram message per 1%+ move), locks in profit (moves stop to break-even), hits brakes on bad days (closes everything if account drops 3% in one day), spots weird behavior (alerts on anomalous sizing/frequency), keeps a log of every move.

When the AI decides to bet against Bitcoin at 3am because it misread a news headline, Monitor catches it in 30 seconds and closes the position.

## Common failure modes

- **Built only the Hunter.** You wake up to "hot picks" and still buy by hand. Same emotional mistakes.
- **Skipped the Analyst.** Hunter finds ideas, Executor blindly buys them, you blow up in week 2.
- **Built Hunter and Executor, no Monitor.** AI runs fine for 10 days, then one bad move at 3am wipes 30% of your account.
- **Used ChatGPT as the Analyst without real testing tools.** Made up fake numbers and indicators.

## Build order

1. Start with Analyst + Executor (Horizon out of the box). Test on paper.
2. Add Monitor. Get Telegram alerts working before live money.
3. Add Hunter last. By then you'll know what setups pass Analyst's filters.
4. Iterate. Most Hunter setups will be rejected. That's correct.

Build the boring layers first. Build the fun layer last.

# Testing Business Ideas as a product operating system

George from prodmgmt.world (@nurijanian)

## Who This Reference Is For

Skip this if you need a beginner's guide to assumption mapping.

This is for product managers who already accept the basic argument: product ideas contain risks; the riskiest assumptions should be made explicit; desirability, feasibility, and viability need to be tested; Test Cards and Learning Cards create discipline; experiments should inform investment decisions.

The useful question: if a PM already buys the testing logic, how do they keep it alive after the workshop ends?

That is where Testing Business Ideas has more depth than people usually remember. The book is often reduced to assumption mapping plus a catalog of experiments. That misses the operating layer: team design, business design, experiment flow, ceremonies, leadership behavior, stakeholder review, and funding.

Most teams do not fail because they cannot write an assumption. They fail because the assumption-mapping workshop produces a nice board, then normal company gravity takes over.

This reference focuses on the parts of the book that stop that from happening. The author also references PM OS (product manager AI operating system with skills, frameworks, workflows, agents, context files in Cursor and Claude Code).

## The Book And Its Real Contribution

Testing Business Ideas — David J. Bland and Alex Osterwalder (Wiley, 2020). Builds on Customer Development / Lean Startup; value is operational.

Four layers: Design (team + shape idea), Test (hypotheses → experiments → decisions), Experiments (library of tests), Mindset (lead so evidence changes strategy and funding).

Treat testing as a managed flow of evidence through a team, into strategy, and back into funding decisions.

## The Missing Mental Model: Search And Testing Before Execution

Separate search/testing from execution. PMs often behave as if executing while still in search (committed roadmap items, untested business cases, one discovery workshop then feature throughput).

If in search: fight for time, focus, evidence flow. If in execution: narrower embedded testing on remaining risk. Do not use the same cadence for both; 0-to-1 vs feature team need different adaptations.

## What Comes Before Assumption Mapping

### Design The Team

Cross-functional team with product, design, technology; dedicated, funded, autonomous. Multitasking across projects silently kills progress.

PM questions: skills to learn and ship tests? real availability? budget for experiments? decision authority?

Assumption mapping without team capacity is theater.

### Shape The Idea: Ideate, Business Prototype, Assess

Do not jump from "we have an idea" to assumptions. Generate strategic options (segments, workflows, service versions, pricing-led versions). Business prototypes (BMC, VPC) make value movement visible. Assess whether this is the best version to test.

Living Assumptions Map — dashboard not workshop photo. Cycle: shape → assumptions → test → learn → update canvases → next test.

## During: Manage Experiments As Flow

Experiment Board: Backlog, Setup, Run, Learn (prefer Learn over Validate to reduce confirmation bias).

WIP limits (e.g. one in Setup, Run, Learn). One important learning thread often enough for real product teams.

## The Ceremonies People Forget

Weekly Planning (what to learn, which decision), Daily Standup on active experiment (or async status/blocker/next/decision date), Weekly Learning (evidence → update BMC/VPC/assumption map → decisions), Biweekly Retrospective (improve the learning system), Monthly Stakeholder Review (confidence change, blockers, pivot/persevere/kill; appendix for Test/Learning cards).

Stakeholder format: decision needed, original belief, evidence, confidence change, blockers, recommendation.

## Funding And Governance

Incremental funding based on learnings; small investment committees with budget authority. PM mimic: fund next test not whole initiative; tie roadmap expansion to evidence strength.

## When Full Cadence Is Realistic vs Fantasy

Full: 0-to-1, new business models, incubation, dedicated discovery funding.

Minimum viable cadence for normal product work: one live learning thread, tiny board, WIP limit, weekly learning moment, monthly stakeholder decision. Embed into backlog refinement, sprint planning/review, monthly product review, roadmap evidence columns.

## Leadership

Strong opinions weakly held; language that lets evidence compete with hierarchy. PM phrases for disprovable hypotheses and staged investment.

## Common 201/301 Failure Modes

Workshop-only maps; experiments without decisions; too many tests in flight; learning without strategy artifact updates; status theater reviews; discovery layered on full delivery; evidence losing to opinion.

## Operating Models

0-to-1: dedicated team + full ceremony stack + staged funding. Normal product team: one thread + embedded rituals + evidence on roadmap items.

## PM Checklist (201/301)

Before: team skills, search vs execution, multiple idea versions, tangible model, funding. During: one learning goal, visible board, WIP, Learn not validate. After: evidence strength, artifact updates, stakeholder decision, persevere/pivot/kill. Across weeks: maps/canvases change, experiments chain, monthly review ties to funding, retro improves process.

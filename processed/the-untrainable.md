# The Untrainable

## Core Thesis

The mid-2026 investor despair — "nothing is investable except Anthropic and Nvidia" — is half right. Thin wrappers are being absorbed. But durable value lives in the "untrainable": frontier work whose correctness can only be verified inside private systems.

## The 2x2 of AI Value

| | Public Correctness | Private Correctness |
|---|---|---|
| **Saturated Tasks** | Commodity tokens (open models win) | — |
| **Frontier Work** | Labs win (coding benchmarks) | **The Prize Corner** (custom models) |

## Why Private Correctness Matters

A benchmark is measurable → trainable → commoditized. But correctness for complex systems (decade-old codebases, M&A deals, clinical decisions) requires running in the real world for years. Noam Brown: evaluating a 1-year-horizon agent may require running it for a year.

## Two Walls Protecting the Untrainable

1. **The Lock (environment):** You only verify usefulness once trusted inside — after security review, integration, contracts with your name on the outcome
2. **The Deadbolt (user):** Majority of US doctors use OpenEvidence daily. No compute buys that. Labs can't gradient-descent into physician habits

## Evidence from Markets

- **Consumer chat:** Best model never simply won. ChatGPT held lead; losing share to Gemini via Android/Search, not model quality. Anthropic (best model per markets) barely a factor
- **Serving:** Not a commodity — reliability under real traffic and guaranteed compute access matter more than per-token price
- **Lab competition:** 3.5-way death match. Labs want market share more than killing any single application

## How Applications Win

Do the unglamorous translation: arrange private reality so models can act on it. Price outcomes (Sierra: pay on resolution; Cognition: performance guarantee). Own the definition of "good" for a field (Harvey for law, Sierra for voice, OpenEvidence for clinical). A foundation lab can't author these benchmarks — that standing only exists inside the field.

## The Absorption Frontier

Keeps rising. Defense: on narrow tasks with private data and custom evals, train specialized models to beat general ones. Offense: choose what to build — the model can't tell you what's worth pointing at. Intent may be scarcer than compute.

# Cracking the AI research interview (Alisa Wuffles)

Harnoor Singh digests Alisa Wuffles’s **Notes on the Industry Job Search** (June 2026): 6-year UW NLP PhD → **Research Scientist / MTS** path, **11 companies / 57 interviews** (plus ~46 recruiter calls and 16 post-offer chats), landed OpenAI. Raw includes full original blog + curated resource links.

## Key takeaways

- **Thesis:** “There is truly no better use of your time than studying for interviews.” Job search ≈ full-time job.
- **Timing:** Practice companies help; stamina is finite — don’t burn out before dream roles. Headcount/team openings often matter more than prep. Exploding offers exist; ask how long candidates usually get to sign.
- **First interview:** Research gets you in the door; **technical skill is evaluated more** than research experience. Referrals and reconnection matter.
- **Interview types:** ML coding (most common; PyTorch fluency; occasional numpy backward pass), general LeetCode, technical discussion (design experiments or rapid-fire breadth: position encodings, 5D parallelism, PPO vs GRPO), research discussion (project pitch tailored to role), behavioral (map PhD stories in advance — easy to blank), math, job talk (shorter, often one paper/thread).
- **Highest-ROI drill:** Implement and debug a **transformer from scratch with AI assistance OFF** (CS336 HW1). Each interview feels like a different midterm with ~3 days to cram.
- **Published study kit:** Alisa’s Book of LLMs; Math notes; Stanford **CS336**; LeetCode 75 / NeetCode Blind 75; Hewitt self-attention notes; Illustrated GPT-2; CS231n backprop; policy gradients for LMs; GRPO guide; JAX scaling book. Also Nathan Lambert’s AI/ML/RL PhD job-hunt post as second data point.
- **Day-of:** Sleep beats last-minute cram; record notes after each interview.
- **Side benefit:** Breadth made her a more confident, effective researcher on the ongoing project.
- **Negotiation:** Initial offers leave room by design; prepare scripts; friends for market data; weeks of energy can equal years of base.
- **Emotional reality:** Stress, comparison, incomplete info for months — not alone; still cherish the PhD as rare idea time.

## Why it matters

Concrete 2026 industry research-interview playbook with open study materials — useful for anyone targeting lab RS/MTS or LLM-systems interviews, not only PhD students.

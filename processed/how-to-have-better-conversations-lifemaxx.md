# How to have better conversations

**@lifemaxxjourney** (lifemaxxers) frames memorable connection as **trainable craft**, not innate charisma — the post-party “I still think about that 20-minute chat” feeling.

## Six habits

1. **Genuine curiosity** — stop drafting your reply; ask what surprised them / almost failed / they’d redo. Removes pressure to perform interestingness.  
2. **Hold opinions loosely** — “I might be wrong” invites equals; certainty grades the other person.  
3. **Common ground before difference** — match **underlying feelings** (outsider, first-time manager) not surface bios.  
4. **Make them feel important** — specific attention (callback to last chat, noticed skill), not flattery.  
5. **Nonverbal majority** — eye contact, open posture, pace, tone of “that sounds hard.”  
6. **Novelty** — break the weather/work/kids rotation with a fresh angle or unasked question.

Closing claim: combine habits → you’re the person people mention on the way home. Soft CTA to lifemaxxers Substack.

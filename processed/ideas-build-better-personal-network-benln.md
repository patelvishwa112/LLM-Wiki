# Ideas to help you build a better personal network — @benln

Rejects vague “build your network” platitudes for 15 tactical habits aimed at goals, not vanity connections.

## Ask and help patterns

1. **No “pick your brain”** — cold outreach: specific ask + why.
2. **Reduce friction** — draft intros, do the lookup work yourself.
3. **Thank-you notes** — short, with a specific conversation detail.
5. **Be useful first** — feedback, intros, shoutouts over extractive framing.
11. **Connector habit** — contact list; forward relevant essays/events/people.
12. **Underpromise** — no inflated closeness or ability claims; add disclaimers.

## Conversation craft

6. **Active listening** — clarify; show you track their goals.
7. **Don’t interrupt** — especially with strangers.
8. **Small details** — hometown, interests; optionally write them down.
9. **Unique questions** — skip generic small talk; ask *why* repeatedly.
10. **Hold judgment** longer — better understanding, stronger ties.

## Character / reputation

4. **No gossip** — negativity and talk about others erodes trust.
13. **Consistency** — rare; long-run brand from steady behavior.
14. **Optimism on others’ work** — hole-poking is easy; path-to-success is valuable (esp. early startups).
15. **Remember your start** — grace for newcomers you once were.

## Why it matters

Operationalizes networking as respect for time + asymmetric helpfulness + memory systems (lists, details). Fits career/platform notes: reputation compounds from small, repeatable behaviors more than from networking “strategy.”

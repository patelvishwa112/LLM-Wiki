# Token Capital Requires Scaffolding, Process, and Governance

**Source:** [X Post by @mardehaym](https://x.com/mardehaym/status/2067547456993177984)

## Summary
Critique and expansion of Satya Nadella's "token capital" concept. Argues that token capital (AI/model investment) only becomes real capital when embedded in scaffolding: process design, governance, knowledge architecture, human judgment, and feedback loops. Presents the multiplication equation **AI Value = Human Capital × Scaffolding × Feedback Loops × Model** and a seven-layer model where most companies skip the foundational layers (process, governance, knowledge) and start at the model, producing non-compounding "AI slop." Includes recent signals from Microsoft, Apple, enterprise surveys, and the Fable 5 shutdown.

## Why It Matters
Reinforces core themes already present in the vault around agent harnesses, scaffolding, feedback loops, and why models alone fail without process architecture. Directly relevant to agent-architecture, harness-engineering, and enterprise AI operationalization discussions. The "draw the process map first" test and "Step Zero" workflow mapping align with existing emphasis on spec-first and process-as-asset thinking. High-signal addition to the compounding-systems and scaffolding cluster.

## Key Concepts
- **Token Capital**: Model investment only becomes capital inside reusable workflows
- **Multiplication Equation**: AI Value = Human Capital × Scaffolding × Feedback Loops × Model (any zero kills the product)
- **Seven Layers**: Process design → Governance → Knowledge architecture → Human judgment → Feedback loops → Scaffolding → Model
- **AI Slop**: Output without feedback/governance that fails to compound
- **Process Map Test**: Can you draw the workflow the agent operates inside?
- **Scaffolding as Moat**: Models are interchangeable; process + governance is not

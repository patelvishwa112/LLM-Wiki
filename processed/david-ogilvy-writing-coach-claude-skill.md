# David Ogilvy Writing Coach (Claude Skill)

**@Nicolascole77** X Article: Ogilvy's one-page **1982 staff memo** as a durable business-writing standard, packaged as a **Claude Skill** that audits social posts, newsletters, and landing pages.

## Thesis for the AI era

You cannot automate writing well if you cannot **articulate** what good means. Ogilvy's rules are the spec; the skill is the linter.

## Ogilvy's ten rules (compressed)

1. Read *Writing That Works* (Roman-Raphaelson) three times.
2. Write like you talk — record → transcribe as draft seed.
3. Short words, sentences, paragraphs; read aloud before publish.
4. No jargon — write for an eighth grader.
5. Cap length at two pages; compress scope if it won't fit.
6. Verify quotations.
7. Sleep on important memos; edit aloud next morning.
8. Peer review on high-stakes pieces.
9. State the **one clear action** you want from the reader.
10. For action that matters, **talk** instead of writing.

## Skill behavior (as described)

- One-line verdict + biggest issue
- Violations tagged Critical / Moderate / Minor with quoted text, why it fails Ogilvy, and a specific fix
- Preserves strengths so edits don't kill what works
- **No auto-rewrite** — human still rewrites to learn

## Skeptical read

Creator-economy lead magnet (free skill via article CTA). Revenue claims ($864M client outcomes) are marketing framing. Rules themselves are mainstream copywriting hygiene—value is **operationalizing** them in a repeatable audit skill.

# How To Actually Design With AI — @LexnLin

Core claim: AI executes design rules (spacing, type, color, hierarchy) but lacks taste — originality, meaning, and what feels good. Humans own idea, direction, feeling, and creativity; AI builds.

## Three methods

### 1. Design skills (fast / decent)

Prebuilt skills (Impeccable, Emil Kowalski UI skills, Skills.sh, TasteSkill; v2 + GPT 5.6 teased) → context → iterate → fix slop → ship. Avoids worst AI defaults; still can feel generic.

### 2. Design it yourself with AI (highest quality)

Intentional pipeline:

1. **Meaning first** — audience, problem, feeling, brand; AI interviews, does not invent the product.
2. **Inspiration library** — Mobbin (primary), Pinterest, Cosmos, Awwwards, Webflow Templates, Craftwork, Rebrand Gallery, Component Gallery, Savee, Lummi. Ask *why* a screen works; folder by pattern (nav, hero, pricing, etc.). Combine, don’t copy.
3. **Map structure** — site sections or app flows with explicit content per block.
4. **Component-by-component** — “hero in this style adapted to my brand,” not “build the whole site.”
5. **Custom assets** — GPT Images 2.0 / Seedream 5.0 with color/style/composition/use-case; avoid stock.

### 3. Inspiration board (balanced)

Curated screenshots + “combine style/direction, do not copy” after full product context and clarifying Q&A.

## Extra tooling

Quiver (SVG/animation), Google Flow (video), Lummi, background removal/layering for depth and motion.

## Why it matters

Frames design skills as a *taste workaround*, not a creativity replacement. Aligns with progressive-disclosure skills practice: smaller tasks beat one-shot full-site prompts. Quality ceiling is the operator’s reference library and intentionality, not prompt cleverness.

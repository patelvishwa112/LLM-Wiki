# Testing Business Ideas as a Product Operating System

George from prodmgmt.world (@nurijanian) — X Article for PMs who already buy desirability/feasibility/viability testing and want the method to survive after the workshop.

## Core thesis

Teams fail when assumption-mapping output is swallowed by roadmap gravity. The book’s depth is the **operating layer**: cross-functional dedicated teams, idea shaping (Ideate → Business Prototype → Assess), experiments as **flow** with WIP limits, and evidence-linked funding—not a one-off Test Card exercise.

## Search vs execution

Treat work explicitly as **search/testing** (high uncertainty) or **execution** (build what you validated). PMs often execute while still in search—committed roadmaps, untested business cases, discovery as a single workshop.

## Experiment board

Columns: Backlog → Setup → Run → **Learn** (not “Validate”). Example WIP: one experiment per active column. Overload kills learning more than missing meetings.

## Ceremonies (ideal vs embedded)

Full cadence: weekly planning, daily experiment standup, weekly learning (update BMC/VPC/assumption map), biweekly retro, monthly stakeholder review (confidence change, pivot/persevere/kill—not activity theater).

**Minimum viable cadence** for delivery-heavy teams: one live learning thread, tiny board, one WIP limit, one weekly learning moment, monthly evidence-based roadmap/stakeholder decision. Attach to existing refinement, sprint review, and product review.

## Governance

Venture-style incremental funding after stakeholder reviews; PMs can mimic with staged asks (“fund the next test,” not the whole initiative).

## Why it matters

Bridges discovery frameworks to **repeatable behavior** under full calendars and politics—complements compression/stakeholder systems in other nurijanian notes and parallels agent **eval/learning loops** (one thread, update artifacts from evidence).

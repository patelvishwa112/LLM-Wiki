# What Is Curiosity? A Bayesian Bandit Perspective

## Summary
@FrancescoSacco1 explores curiosity mathematically through the lens of the K-armed bandit problem and Bayesian inference. The core insight: curiosity is what optimal action looks like when your beliefs are uncertain.

## Key Takeaways
- **Mathematical definition of curiosity:** Optimal action under uncertainty — when you don't know which arm is best, the rational move is to explore
- **Bayesian bandit framework:** Curiosity emerges naturally from Bayesian decision theory when prior beliefs are uncertain
- **Practical results:** The same math is being applied to build a chess engine with promising results

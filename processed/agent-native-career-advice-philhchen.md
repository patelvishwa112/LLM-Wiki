# Agent-native career advice (Phil Chen)

**@philhchen** X Article from a founder hiring at a **fully agent-native** company (ex-Helm, Scale, OpenAI, Google).

## Opening thesis

Models excel where **loss functions** exist (school-shaped tasks). Next-decade value sits in work that **cannot be graded within a training horizon**—problem framing, taste, relationships, capital allocation.

## Six hiring/career pillars

1. **Limited resources** — Time, relationships, reputation > cash; visible excellence among reputable peers; meaningful problems over vibe-coded arbitrage.
2. **Find problems, not only solve** — Interviews emphasize environment comprehension, problem selection, execution under constraints; agent collaboration efficiency + outside context still differentiates candidates.
3. **Ambitious problem forms** — Power-law careers accelerated; pick frontier roles and companies attacking the hardest version of their market.
4. **Last mile** — Polish separates median agent slop from durable value (ties to Alfred Lin last-10% framing); iterate/restart with newer models.
5. **xG + efficiency** — Create high-quality opportunities (network, expertise) and convert them; early-stage = team + market over demo product.
6. **Research path** — Evals, leaderboards, academic compute credits; researcher as mentality (curiosity, infra, systems debugging, compute advocacy).

## Skeptical read

Anecdotal career narrative and recruiting CTA; DSA skeptics in replies note compsci fluency still matters for mishire risk.

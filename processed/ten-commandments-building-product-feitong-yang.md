# The Ten Commandments for Building Product

By Feitong Yang (@feitong_yang), founding engineer on **Shortcut** (Excel coworker) after pivots through **Fairies** (general desktop agent) and a Minecraft coplayer.

## Central thesis

Pre-PMF building fails when you **avoid painful first-hand contact with reality**. AI makes this worse: cheap code, agents, and “correct architecture” feel like extraordinary productivity while you stand still.

## The ten (compressed)

| # | Commandment | Punchline |
|---|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Honest commitment vs love of idea | Cannot delegate user pain, rejection, uncertainty |
| 2 | Self-expression ≠ user knowledge | Taste is hypothesis until users endorse |
| 3 | Don't pretend to know user | “Developers” / “Excel users” are not users; sit with specifics |
| 4 | Breakthrough ≠ product | Capability needs translation (ChatGPT box, Cursor, Claude Code) |
| 5 | Don't offload “how to use” | Fairies’ blank box failed; Shortcut’s spreadsheet affordance worked |
| 6 | Not every feature equal | Say no; “one more feature” is anxiety from unnamed user |
| 7 | Outcomes not activity | Line count / vibe-coded PRs ≠ retention or payment |
| 8 | Not a million before a thousand | Scale early is engineer-comfort procrastination |
| 9 | Product doesn't sell itself | Builder-as-missionary; distribution often beats superiority |
| 10 | Patience — spark ≠ fuel | Viral launch spikes; Humane/Rabbit vs Cursor/Claude Code/OpenClaw |

## Why it matters (agent / AI builder lens)

Directly challenges **agent-first** and **harness-first** optimism: scaffolding truth before anyone wants it is the same illusion as logs instead of watching users. Pairs with product discipline for anyone shipping Hermes skills, internal agents, or interpretability tooling—not just SaaS founders.

Case thread: **Shortcut** found fit in high-caliber finance Excel work; **Fairies** died on unclear wedge; Minecraft agent won attention without clear need.

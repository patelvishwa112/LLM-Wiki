# Effort is Linear, Position is Exponential — Five Principles of Market Reality

**Source:** [X Post by @incentivising](https://x.com/incentivising/status/2068248923563573366)

## Summary

A recurring DM ("I put in a ton of effort but never get anywhere") prompted this essay on why raw effort frequently fails to produce proportional results in modern markets.

**Core thesis:** You are not lazy, undisciplined, or deficient. You are missing **knowledge of mechanisms**. The industrial-era model ("work hard → rewarded") no longer holds because institutions no longer teach the real rules.

### The Five Principles

1. **Effort is Linear, Position is Exponential (Leverage)**  
   Effort scales linearly. Position, network, and leverage scale exponentially. Most people optimize the wrong variable.

2. **Invisible Competence Is Just a Hobby (Costly Signals)**  
   Competence that cannot be credibly signaled (Spence 1973) remains private and unrewarded. Costly signals are required.

3. **The Market Is Indifferent to Effort**  
   Markets price outcomes and scarcity, not hours. Effort without position or the right problem is invisible.

4. **Most People Are Solving The Wrong Problem**  
   Enormous disciplined effort is wasted on low-leverage or misdiagnosed problems.

5. **Time Spent is a Cost. Results Are the Return**  
   Institutions still reward inputs (time, credentials). The real game rewards outputs and asymmetric position.

References: Rosen (1981) on superstar markets, Spence signaling, Ericsson deliberate practice.

## Why It Matters

Directly relevant to career transition strategy (AI interpretability/safety roles, Austin preference, visa sponsorship needs). Explains why traditional "grind harder" advice fails and why position, signaling, and problem selection dominate outcomes. Complements agentic career planning and the shift toward AI-native roles where leverage is extreme.

Strong connection to second-brain / personal effectiveness systems and the "bitter lesson" style thinking about what actually compounds.

## Key Takeaways for Career Builders
- Optimize for position and leverage first
- Make competence visible and costly to fake
- Choose problems the market actually values
- Track results, not time or effort

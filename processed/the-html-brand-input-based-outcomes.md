# The HTML Brand: The Rise of Input-Based Outcomes

## Key Takeaways

- **Value is moving upstream.** The highest-value work is no longer the final output — it's the strategic judgment encoded into agent-readable systems. Positioning, voice, audience definition — these judgment calls are the product.
- **"Atomic kits" as deliverables.** Instead of PDFs and Figma files, agencies ship structured directories of YAML, JSON, Markdown, HTML, CSS, and SVG. A brand encoded as instructions agents can build from, not documentation humans read.
- **The magic_trick.md pattern.** When everything is agentic, outputs converge toward the median. The human creative spark — the weird, sweaty, original idea — is what tilts outcomes. The system produces correctness; humans produce originality. You need both.
- **HTML is the agent-native format.** HTML, CSS, and SVG are instructions agents can act on directly. Flat raster exports (PNG, JPEG, PDF) can only be viewed. Build for how agents read and construct, not just how humans view.
- **Pricing is shifting to software economics.** The future is hybrid: scoped time-and-materials upfront, ongoing licensing for the living systems left behind. "Aim to be paid for your mind, not your hands."
- **Brand as a world model.** Echoing Jack Dorsey's "From Hierarchy to Intelligence" thesis: an atomic kit is a structured, machine-readable understanding of the brand. The depth of that understanding is the moat.

## Summary

Emmett Shine and Alex Leiphart of Little Plains describe a fundamental shift in creative agency work: deliverables are no longer static assets but "atomic kits" — structured, agent-readable file systems that AI tools (Claude Design, Cursor, Codex) can ingest and build from. A single prompt produces a full landing page with correct branding, motion, and copy.

The breakthrough is enabled by Claude Design, which lets them work visually, verbally, and structurally in the same tool. 12-week engagements compress to 4 weeks without compressing value — the value actually increases.

Their key insight: the agentic system handles correctness and consistency, but only human creativity (what they call magic_trick.md) can produce the left-of-center moves that make work memorable. They use Diplo's concept of "vocabulary" — accumulated experience and taste — as the analogy. The atomic kit is the record library; magic_trick.md is knowing which record to play on which night.

The business model is evolving from time-and-materials toward software-style licensing. The upfront strategy work concentrates the value; the ongoing relationship is about maintaining access to a living, improving system.

# I looked at 1,680 Anthropic resumes. Here's who they actually hire.

Analysis of current Anthropic engineers (LinkedIn data) reveals a clear picture of what the lab actually values at the engineering level.

## Key Stats
- **Scale & Tenure**: Org roughly tripled in 2025. 53% joined in the last 12 months. Median tenure: 10 months.
- **Experience**: Median 12.2 years prior experience. Only 50 of 1,680 have <3 years. New-grad hiring is essentially nonexistent.
- **Background**: 40% infra (backend, distributed systems, databases, security). RL shows up in only 3.3%.
- **Feeder Companies**: Google (405) dominates. Other strong sources: Meta, Amazon, Microsoft, Stripe, Databricks, Snowflake, Palantir, Airbnb. ~50% have FAANG on resume.
- **Education**: Only 13.7% hold a PhD. Fields: Computer Science (819), Math, Physics. Stanford (144), Berkeley (118), MIT (80) lead.
- **Title**: 80% are simply "Member of Technical Staff" — seniority is deliberately flattened.

## Junior Entry Points (the exception)
172 engineers with <6 years experience. They differ sharply from the senior cohort:
- Higher PhD rate (19%)
- More product/SWE titles
- Less FAANG
- Entry via: elite internships (Meta, Google, DeepMind, Jane Street, etc.), quant trading shops, or alignment fellowships (MATS, SERI, Redwood, ARC)
- Often screened on competition rank (IOI, Codeforces) and publications

## Implications
For joining Anthropic as an engineer: write the resume for an **infra company**, not a research lab. Show systems you built and scaled.

For hiring against them: target senior builders from hyperscalers or infra-reputation shops (Stripe, Databricks, Snowflake, Palantir), not PhDs or lab logos.

Relevant to career transition planning and understanding frontier lab engineering DNA.

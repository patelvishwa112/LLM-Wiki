# Every Agentic Engineering Hack I Know (June 2026)

## Key Takeaways

- **plan.md is the universal interface.** Write a plan before any task — coding, strategy, research, life decisions. Don't read it yourself after writing; let the agent execute it. Voice-dictate plans for speed. Share plans with colleagues instead of meetings.
- **Terminal as agent, not shell.** Default terminal opens into Claude/Codex, not bash. Run parallel agents in cmux tabs. Skip permission prompts — the friction kills flow. Give agents email and browser access so they interact with the world.
- **Notes as knowledge base, transcripts as context.** Feed raw meeting transcripts directly to agents — don't summarize first, let the agent extract what matters. Your accumulated notes (Obsidian, etc.) become instantly queryable context. Record everything with Granola.
- **Skills compound.** Write custom skills for your specific workflows. Skills are the compound interest of agentic engineering — each one makes every future task faster. Matt contributes to CPython and Go through agent-assisted workflows.
- **Accept AI psychosis as part of the process.** Agents sometimes go off the rails — creating files you didn't ask for, making nonsensical changes. Learn to recognize and recover rather than trying to eliminate it entirely. Human signal checkpoints keep you in control.

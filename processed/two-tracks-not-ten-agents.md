# You don't need ten agents. You need two tracks.

**Author:** Hugo Baraúna (@hugobarauna)  
**Format:** X post sharing external article (link + preview)

## Core Thesis
The hype around running 10+ coding agents in parallel for productivity is misleading. Instead of scaling the number of agents, focus on "two tracks": one for exploration/ideation and one for execution/verification. This structured approach outperforms brute-force parallelism.

## Why It Matters
- Strongly aligns with vault themes in agent architecture, harness engineering, and multi-agent orchestration.
- Challenges the "more agents = better" narrative seen in recent demos; emphasizes quality of workflow design over quantity.
- Complements existing notes on agent loops, harnesses, and practical agent-ops patterns.
- High-signal reminder that orchestration and track separation (e.g., research vs. implementation) are key to effective agent systems.

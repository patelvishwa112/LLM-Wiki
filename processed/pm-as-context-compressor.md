# The PM as the Context Compressor

By George from prodmgmt.world (@nurijanian)

## Core Insight

**Information should compress upward, not downward.** The PM is the compression layer between execs and engineering. Half-formed thoughts from leadership are more dangerous than silence — they give just enough to confidently go in the wrong direction, costing sprints of wasted work.

This is a structural problem, not a communication problem. The fix isn't more face time — it's a system.

## The System

Three context files:
- **STAKEHOLDERS.md** — execs' metrics, concerns, tradeoff thinking
- **GOALS.md** — what decisions are being framed at what altitude
- **COMPANY.md** — organizational context for flagging misalignment

Two PM OS skills:
- **/meeting** — generates single-paragraph brief tailored to a stakeholder's context (what's happening, why it matters, what you need)
- **/review** — Multi-Perspective Review that pressure-tests framing before it leaves your desk

Compresses 2 days of triangulation into a 1-paragraph brief delivered before the meeting.

## The Cost

One day/week deciphering = 10 weeks/year in ambiguity, plus 4-6 sprints delivering the wrong thing. Two months of your year burned on a structural problem you can fix.

# On mid-career (dis)satisfaction

## Key Takeaways

- Career satisfaction in mid-career is not about your LinkedIn — it's about your ability to combat career envy
- People judge your career by Title, Money, Scope (#6). No one judges by Competence, Flow, Culture fit, Work-life harmony, Sunday evening feeling (#7)
- #6 makes you happy when you accept the offer. #7 is what keeps you happy after day one. We all know this but reliably fail at acting on it
- The moment you make your career identity about #6, you've made a deal with the devil
- "Your career is not a performance you are doing for an audience" — the imagined audience doesn't know you, understand you, or care about you. Their judging is just soothing their own insecurities
- The only true audience: you and those dependent on you
- This problem is getting worse with polarized AI outcomes

## Why This Matters

- You're mid-career in tech during the AI boom — career envy is a real risk, especially given your interpretability/safety career transition goal
- The framing of #6 vs #7 is a useful filter for evaluating any career move (including your Sept 2027 transition)
- The reminder that "you are the user of your career" aligns with the self-awareness you already practice

## Summary

Shreyas Doshi (ex-Stripe/Twitter/Google PM turned advisor) distills career satisfaction for ambitious mid-career tech people into 12 observations. Core insight: external metrics (title, money, scope) produce short-lived happiness; internal metrics (competence, flow, fit, harmony) sustain it. The problem is getting worse with polarized AI outcomes. The way out: recognize your career is not a performance for an audience that doesn't care about you.

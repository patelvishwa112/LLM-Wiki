# Post-Agent Companies

**Source:** [X Post by @jrwoodbridge](https://x.com/jrwoodbridge/status/2067985204958880038) | Co-authored with [@yrechtman](https://x.com/yrechtman) | Cross-posted to [Substack: Eyes on the End Game](https://99d.substack.com/p/eyes-on-the-end-game)

## Summary

Justin Woodbridge outlines the "Post-Agent Company" as the next evolutionary stage beyond current AI agent applications. As AI models make cognitive labor near-free and infinite, successful companies will commoditize labor (give digital workers away for free) to capture the remaining scarce resources: context, attention, trust, brand, and network effects.

Current paradigms ("system of action" vertical SaaS or "neofirms"/AI rollups) are transitional. Post-agent companies act as asset-light attention aggregators and agent-native marketplaces, monetizing via transactions and networks rather than software licenses. The entire organization runs on internal agent harnesses, with small, highly technical/entrepreneurial teams.

**Phoebe** (in-home healthcare) serves as the canonical example: specialized agents for scheduling, talent matching, and care advising, with a central "Phoebe Core" agent system handling operations.

## Why It Matters

This framework provides a clear lens for evaluating which AI companies and strategies will compound in the long term versus those optimized for short-term margins. It emphasizes building proprietary context and network effects through free labor distribution — a pattern that aligns with open-source model trends and agent orchestration systems. Directly relevant to agent-harness design, multi-agent workflows, and the shift from tool-selling to system-building.

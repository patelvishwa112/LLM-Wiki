# The Economy of Tokens

Long-form X Article from **Vipul Ved Prakash** (CEO, Together AI). Core frame: **Baldwin & Clark modularity** (stable interfaces → explosive ecosystem innovation) is replaying in AI.

## Three standardized interfaces

1. **Transformer architecture** — shared industrial substrate (kernels, vLLM/SGLang, Megatron/FSDP, silicon).
2. **OpenAI-compatible inference APIs** — fungible token consumption.
3. **Agent harnesses** — loops that turn tokens into work; **MCP** and emerging **Skills** shapes as coordination standards.

## Why open weights win economically

- Recipe diffusion (RoPE, GQA, MoE, GRPO/R1), not just distillation.
- Together cites **10,000×** API token growth (30B → 400T/month) and agents in production.
- Open tokens **>10× cheaper** than closed frontier; agentic I/O ratios (~300:1) widen the gap; hybrid routing is already normal.
- Revenue and VC validation (Moonshot, MiniMax, Mistral, Chinese labs, Reflection, NVIDIA Nemotron as complement commoditization).

## Software industrialization

SWE-bench ~2% → >85%; software engineering competence spreads across the wider open frontier, not only closed labs.

## Infrastructure & politics

Neoclouds, GPU-backed financing, sovereign AI — stack disaggregation mirrors PC era. **Sun-style premium collapse** when open is "good enough" on a workload class. Biggest risk is **regulatory capture** framing openness as unsafe—not economics.

## Why it matters

Connects **token economics**, **agent harness/MCP** standardization, and **open-weights strategy** in one operator-scale narrative (400T tokens/month). Pairs with investing essays on who prices intelligence and enterprise moats.

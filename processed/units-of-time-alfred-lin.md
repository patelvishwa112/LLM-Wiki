# Units of Time

## Key Takeaways

- **Three units of time: moment, chapter, book.** The moment is finite and irreplaceable. The chapter is your choice — what period marks a significant milestone. The book is your legacy — the story told at the end. "The days are long, but the years are short."
- **The game has gotten 10x harder in the same fixed time.** Most valuable companies went from $300B to $4-5T in 15 years. Human lifespan hasn't moved. We must cover 10x the distance with the same moments, chapters, and pages. The question isn't how to work more hours — it's which moments are worth writing at all.
- **The opportunity cost of attention has never been higher.** Every choice to work on one thing is a choice not to work on something else. The best founders find the one or two questions whose answers can change paradigms. They compound 1% daily and sign up for the long game.
- **Steve Jobs framing: follow your heart and intuition.** "They somehow already know what you truly want to become. Everything else is secondary." The moment is finite. The chapter is your choice. The book is your legacy.

## Summary

Alfred Lin (Sequoia partner who backed Airbnb, DoorDash, etc.) answers his son's question about time with a three-unit framework: moment (irreplaceable seconds), chapter (milestone-defining periods), and book (life's legacy). He adds a pragmatic observation: today's builders face 10x the ambition in the same fixed human lifespan. The response isn't to grind more hours — it's to be radically selective about what earns your attention, compound daily, and play the long game.

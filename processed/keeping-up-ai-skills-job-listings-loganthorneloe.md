# How I keep up with important AI skills (Logan Thorneloe)

Logan Thorneloe (@loganthorneloe) — newsletter-style X post on **how to choose what to learn** when AI moves faster than any one curriculum.

## 1. Job listings as skill radar

Treat postings as three layers:

| Section | Use |
|---------|-----|
| About the company | Critical path, goals, culture red flags |
| About the role | Real day-to-day (inference vs training infra, etc.) |
| **Qualifications** | **Paid-for skills** — durable signal vs social-media fads |

Track frontier companies even when not job-hunting; build a **monitor** that surfaces in-demand skills for your target role profile.

## 2. Learning resources

- **RSS** — slim, high-signal feeds; daily agent-filtered email digest (noise vs importance)  
- **X + Substack** — discourse vs long-form depth  
- **DM experts** — ask for reading lists; low spam risk  

Curate feeds deliberately (e.g. Readwise Reader).

## 3. Learning posture

Map the full stack (hardware → research → infra → apps) but **don’t try to learn everything**. Combine market signal with **genuine interest** and prior experience; go deep on one wedge.

## Why it matters

Practical complement to roadmap posts: **qualifications sections** as ground truth for skill investment, plus a repeatable information diet — aligns with career-transition and cron job-scanning patterns.

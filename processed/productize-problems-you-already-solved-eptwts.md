# Productize problems you already solved (EP)

@eptwts argues the common online-business blocker is not "how to build" but **which problems are worth solving**. People know business sells solutions, yet lack a method. Claim: almost everyone has already fixed productizable problems — the work is to inventory them, not invent outsider problems.

## Why solved problems win

- Pain understanding (lived)
- Known-working solution
- Proof via own results
- Authentic explanation (your story)

Outsider "I think this exists" bets risk building what nobody wants. Best solutions usually come from people **deep inside a community** who already live the pain and talk to others who share it.

## Four-step inventory

1. **Achievements** — money made, time saved, systems built, obstacles beaten, results others still miss. Dump everything (agency clients, diet framework, AI automation hours, traffic from "dead" platforms, email growth, cold DM reply systems).
2. **Problems overcome per achievement** — initial blocker, months of struggle, breakthrough, what others still miss. Example: first client → couldn't find clients → niche authority → others stay too broad.
3. **Money / time / status filter** — people care about making money, saving money, saving time, status/attraction. Delete pure "nice to have."
4. **Surface vs root** — what buyers *think* the problem is vs the real underlying problem. Often **sell surface, solve root** (e.g. "need more clients" vs "can't position as authority so clients come to you").

Target output: **3–5** lived-and-solved problems others still struggle with, already mapped to money/time/status.

If empty: join communities with shared pain and solve something, **or** affiliate existing products after deep problem study (still weaker than lived expertise).

## Pre-productize checks

- **Pain intensity** — mildly annoying ≠ buyers; kidney-level pain does.
- **One-sentence transformation** — "The [PROBLEM] was costing me [PAIN]. I discovered [SOLUTION], and now [OUTCOME]." If you can't say it simply, you don't understand it yet.

## Failure mode

Skipping research and jumping to building produces solutions to problems nobody has — not because the product is bad, but because the problem was unlived, disconnected from core desires, or not painful enough to pay for.

Unfair advantage of starting from **your** problems: authenticity, proof, understanding, head start.

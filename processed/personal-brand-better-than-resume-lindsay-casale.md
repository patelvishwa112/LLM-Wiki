# Your Personal Brand is Better Than a Resume

@LindsayCasale (fractional CMO for early-stage tech founders) — practical playbook for personal branding as a durable career asset.

## Origin story (proof of work)

College job-hunt advice from an Oracle exec: start a **blog** that shows expertise — not just resume/LinkedIn. She ran **Rising Pro** (WordPress) for **10 years**, writing in real time about early SF career (salary negotiation, bosses, promotions). Outcomes claimed: proof of writing, design, SEO, social amplification, marketer’s thinking → career leverage (Salesforce, SoFi, venture-backed startups); still relevant 16 years later because most people still do the bare minimum. Tools now easier (Substack/Medium; vibe-code a site in an afternoon).

## Definition

Define expertise → communicate clearly → share consistently → reputation that **pulls opportunities over time**.

## Positioning first (5 questions)

Treat yourself like a company launch before content:

1. Who do you want to help?  
2. What do you want to be known for?  
3. What perspective do others lack?  
4. What evidence backs that?  
5. If someone recommended you, what’s the **one sentence** you’d want them to say?

## Four build steps

1. **Messaging** — Translate positioning into repeatable language; test/refine; use as bio, site headline, elevator pitch, content topics  
2. **Digital presence** — Home base: prefer **personal site + blog**; social drives traffic back  
3. **Content engine** — Few themes; ~**3×/week social** + **1–2 blogs/month**; depth > breadth  
4. **Networking & distribution** — Conversations, speaking, podcasts, communities where the audience already is  

Consistency compounds positioning + content + visibility into reputation that attracts work.

## Visibility without drowning

Fear of being front-and-center is the real hurdle. **Start small, stack reps:** GTM Tea started with Twitter Spaces (audio, low stakes) → multi-network camera livestreams; speaking from peer events → larger stages.

## Why it matters

Frames personal brand as **auditable body of work** and distribution system (platform/email-list cousins), not vanity. Useful checklist for anyone building public professional surface area.

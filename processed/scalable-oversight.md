# Scalable Oversight

How do we supervise systems that are smarter than the supervisor? Scalable
oversight is the research program looking for training and evaluation
protocols that remain trustworthy even when human judgement is no longer
ground truth — debate, weak-to-strong generalization, recursive reward
modeling, and decomposed evaluation.

## What the sources say

- [[arxiv-2211.03540]] — "Measuring Progress on Scalable Oversight for
  LLMs": Anthropic's empirical benchmark
- [[arxiv-2312.09390]] — "Improving Weak-to-Strong Generalization":
  using weak supervisors to elicit strong-model capabilities reliably
- [[arxiv-2210.10760]] — "Reward Modeling for Mitigating Overoptimization
  in RLHF": directly relevant failure mode
- [[arxiv-2211.00593]] — Circuit-level analysis as a substrate for
  oversight techniques

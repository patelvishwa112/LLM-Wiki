# Recursive Self-Improvement

## Key Takeaways

1. **AI already accelerates AI development.** >80% of Anthropic's merged code is Claude-authored (May 2026). Engineers ship 8x more code per day vs 2024. The trendlines are exponential, not linear.

2. **The doing costs almost nothing.** Writing code, running experiments, producing results — human time cost is near zero. The bottleneck moved to reviewing, judging, and direction-setting. Amdahl's law now applies organizationally.

3. **Task horizons are doubling every 4 months.** 4 min (Mar 2024) → 1.5 hrs → 12 hrs → 16+ hrs. By 2027: tasks taking weeks. SWE-bench and CORE-bench both saturated within 2 years.

4. **Code quality trajectory is unambiguous.** "Worse than human in late 2025 → parity today → strictly better within the year." Automated Claude code review would have caught 1/3 of past claude.ai incidents.

5. **Research automation is superhuman.** Training optimization: 52x speedup vs human ceiling of 4x. Open-ended research: Claude agents recovered 97% of weak→strong gap vs humans at 23%.

6. **Three futures, one most likely.** The trend could stall (S-curve / supply chain), compound efficiently (most likely per evidence), or achieve full recursive self-improvement. Anthropic supports verified multilateral pause but acknowledges verification is harder than nuclear arms control.

7. **Research taste is the last human advantage.** Claude beats human next-step decisions 64% of the time (Mythos Preview), up from 51% (Opus 4.5). The gap is closing.

## The Critical Threshold

The transition from human-written to human-reviewed to human-directed code is the arc of the entire piece. When code quality reaches "strictly better than human," the economic logic flips: humans become the bottleneck. The organization's limiting factor shifts from production to judgment.

# The goldmine business of selling data to frontier labs

Vikram Aditya (@viks_rum) maps the boom in companies selling human training data to frontier labs — huge one-off purchase orders, tiny teams, almost no recurring revenue — and asks what the cash should actually buy before the treadmill moves on.

## Key takeaways

- **Category is six products, not one:** hours (dying crowd labeling), judgment (credentialed experts at high hourly rates), worlds/environments (simulated enterprise tools + rubrics + verifiers), verdicts (benchmarks/evals/red teams), bodies (physical sensor capture for robotics), rights (licensed archives / data annuities).
- **Revenue mechanics matter more than headlines:** almost everything is a PO against a deliverable; nothing renews by default; gross ≠ net (marketplaces pass through ~60–70%; some offshore delivery ops keep 70–80%+).
- **Accidental giants:** Mercor, Micro1, Turing, Handshake (and Scale earlier) won because they already verified strangers at scale — verified judgment was the product, not “data.”
- **Why labs keep paying:** no durable capability lead; data spend is a tax on nobody winning. Each model generation graduates past prior data (crowd → experts → environments). Buyers multi-source the same work and actively build in-house replacements (platforms, specialist tutors, environment frameworks).
- **Environments economics:** simple website replicas ~$20k one-shot; high-fidelity enterprise clones much higher; exclusivity multiplies 4–5×; tasks depreciate once models pass ~70% — homework that graduates every quarter.
- **Law of the market:** whatever machines can verify, they eventually learn without vendors; taste, ambiguity, regulated judgment, and the physical world stay scarce longest.
- **Capital allocation menu:** go deep not wide (own one hard domain’s best experts); own full physical capture loops (industrial settings still empty); sell environments up the stack as enterprise private gyms; treat enterprise agent deploy as a separate business; acquire next rungs with cash; sell sovereign/gov data; convert some POs into renewals (eval subs, maintenance, refresh retainers). Avoid generalist lane vs giants and vanity raises that close optionality.
- **Trust compounds:** security/residency certs, provenance (camera-verified sessions, credential attestation), neutrality covenants, public benchmarks, expert certification brands transfer when formats change every ~2 years.
- **5-year view:** gross market keeps growing but composition rotates violently; hours mostly dead; generic environments commoditize; value concentrates in frontier judgment + provenance, enterprise private gyms, physical capture, referees. Expect fewer than ~10 independent scaled vendors by 2031. Closing line: gold learns to mine itself — survivors sell “what does good look like?” in a narrow domain.

## Why it matters

Sharp operator-level map of AI data markets for anyone tracking lab spend, evals/environments, or agent training infrastructure. Separates fragile PO revenue from durable assets (verification, trust, private gyms, physical loops) and connects to enterprise evals/harness demand beyond the five frontier buyers.

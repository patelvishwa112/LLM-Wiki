# Principled Thinking and AI Need to Go Together

Ray Dalio on merging human and artificial intelligence through explicit, systemized principles rather than blind reliance on either.

## Core Idea
Effective intelligence comes from **principled thinking** — examining and systemizing decision-making criteria based on logical understandings of how reality works (cause → effect), then writing those principles down so they can be applied, backtested, and paired with AI.

## The Process
- Study circumstances and the criteria used to make decisions in those circumstances.
- Write explicit "if this happens, do this because XYZ" statements.
- Backtest the criteria across time, environments, and countries to ensure they are timeless and universal.
- Computerize and automate the principles.
- Use AI as a partner: the system explains its logic; you compare, debate, and align. For non-systemizable criteria, use qualitative ratings and discretionary overrides.

## Why It Matters
- AI alone lacks the unique human insights needed in zero-sum domains like investing.
- Data mining or simply "asking an AI what to do" is insufficient.
- The goal is mutual learning between human and AI, with reasoning always transparent.

This approach powered Bridgewater's success and is now being evolved with modern AI at the Dalio Family Office. Dalio believes those who master this will stay competitive; others will not.

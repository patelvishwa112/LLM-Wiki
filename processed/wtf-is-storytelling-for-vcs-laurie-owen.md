# WTF is Storytelling for VCs?

@imlaurieowen (Laurie Owen, Refinery Media) — frameworks for fund narrative after the “storyteller” hiring wave and a16z-style content arms race. Focus: **how storytelling works for VC firms**, not companies.

## Zoom in vs zoom out

| | Companies | VCs |
|--|-----------|-----|
| Judgment horizon | Near/mid-term execution | Multiple vintages / decade |
| Narrative zoom | Specific problem, customer, wedge, roadmap | World change, where value accrues, compounding decisions |
| What narrative proxies | Product-market fit path | Ability to deliver returns over a decade vs “well-packaged tourists” |

Because VC success is lagging, funds use narrative + **evidence-in-progress** (markups, follow-ons, founder refs, internal discipline) to bridge hypothesis → DPI.

## Story vs messaging

Real story needs **character, conflict, stakes, resolution** + emotion. Messaging alone (differentiation claims, portfolio lists, partner bios) skips the structure.

For VCs the character may be portfolio founder, the future, or the market itself — structure stays fixed.

## Build the fund story

1. **Delete wallpaper** — founding origin fluff, every “we believe,” generic “add value / partner with founders”
2. Ask what the fund is really trying to do for **founders, LPs, the world**
3. Fill character / conflict / stakes / resolution with a **specific, visible future** (example: climate fund before/after — virtue-signalling mission vs scene of driving past a former coal plant in 2035)

Repeat the same spine across deck, site, quarterly LP comms, deal conversations — not separate “content tasks.”

## Four frames that zoom out

### 1. Contrast (Villain → Hero)

Template: most VCs promise X but deliver Y; we built to do the opposite.

**Example:** Founders Fund — flying cars vs 140 characters; “Bad/Easy” quests; villain = timid incrementalism.

Craft: sharp contrast + clear enemy. “We’re different” is empty; “we fund what others call crazy” is legible.

### 2. Insight (Mystery → Discovery → Application)

Template: working with [founder type], we noticed [pattern] → [approach].

**Example:** YC — great startups don’t look great early; speed/iteration/founder quality > polish/pedigree → batch model, light applications, “launch now.”

Craft: counter-intuitive pattern recognition hammered for decades until it *is* the operating model.

### 3. Solution (Current state → Future state)

Template: founders waste hours on [problem]; we built [resource/approach] to remove it. Or: world broken at [institutions]; we back rebuilders.

**Example:** Families Fund — education + health under pressure; back founders rebuilding those institutions.

Craft: problem + mission others can retell (not just thesis jargon).

### 4. Path (Fork → two journeys → outcomes)

Template: typical VC capital → [bad journey]; with us → [different journey] because [specific difference].

**Examples:** Indie.vc (raise-scale-exit vs revenue-based / long-term ownership); Terrain “Call Your Shot” vs generic Startup Track.

Craft: feel of the journey, not only backward prestige or “enduring companies.”

## Craft levers

- **Specificity** (unresponsive VCs only reply when they need LP financials)
- **Contrast** (before/after scenes)
- **Stakes** (“if we don’t build it now, nobody else will”)
- **Time** (decade of pattern recognition language)

## Bottom line

Don’t hire a “storyteller” as a trend substitute for clarity. Pick **one** structure, take a position, name the villain/problem, paint a specific future, weave it everywhere until others can tell it for you. **Zoom out. Build. Repeat.**

## Why it matters

Operationalizes fund differentiation as narrative structure (not feature lists) — useful for reading LP/GP materials and for any multi-decade thesis (including AI investing) that must survive lagging proof.

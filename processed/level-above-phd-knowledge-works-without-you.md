# There's a Level Above PhD: Knowledge That Works Without You

**114 bookmarks, 295K views.** The old ladder rewarded being the best calculator. AI made that worth zero. The new ladder rewards ideas, judgment, and taste — and it starts where you stop doing the work yourself.

## The Two Ladders

| Old Ladder | New Ladder |
|------------|------------|
| Study harder, compute faster, publish more | Ideas, judgment, taste |
| Be a better machine than other humans | Be the one who imagines what's worth calculating |
| The mule always won | Get off the mule |

**Core insight:** You were never valuable because you could calculate. A calculator can calculate. You were valuable because you could imagine what was worth calculating. The old ladder paid you for the wrong half of yourself.

## Digitize Yourself

Extract everything from your head: methods, rules of thumb, decision trees, the reasons behind your reasons. Most experts have never written this down. It lives as instinct.

> "That isn't notes. It's a copy of your judgment."

## The System

1. **Create a Claude Project** for your domain. Load it with your papers, methods, decision rules, quality checks. Custom instructions: always show assumptions, flag what you'd reject, write in your voice.

2. **Build the corpus:** "Read everything in this Project. Map how I make decisions in this field. Write it so a sharp junior could make my calls."

3. **Automate the ideas:**
   - "Give me 30 hypotheses my field hasn't tested. Rank by novelty × feasibility."
   - Adversarial review: one chat generates, a second attacks — "Find the fatal flaw in each. Which three survive?"
   - Web search for frontier gaps: "Show me contradictions and open questions."
   - Cross-domain transplant: "Take the mechanism from this other field and map it onto my problem."

4. **Automate refinement:** Steelman → Break → Fix → Design cheapest falsification experiment → List every failure mode.

5. **Split the roles:** Generator / Critic / Executor — keep them separate. You are the **editor-in-chief**. You don't produce. You judge. You point: deeper, kill it, yes.

6. **Compounding memory file:** Every good call, every "never again," every discovered rule — feed it back in. The system gets more like you every week.

## The Timeline

- **Week 1:** Faster assistant
- **Month 1:** Runs routine work without you
- **Month 3:** Haven't done a calculation by hand in weeks. More real ideas than the year before. The grunt work didn't speed up — it left.

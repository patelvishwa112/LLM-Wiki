# Priests of AGI

By Julius Adebayo (@juliusadml) | May 14, 2026 | 17.6K views | 99 bookmarks

## Key Takeaways

- **Faithfulness crisis:** Every generation of interpretability tools (saliency maps → probes → SAEs → activation oracles) mistakes human legibility for genuine model understanding
- **Saliency maps:** Randomizing model weights produces nearly identical heatmaps — the relic glowed after the bones were removed
- **Probes:** Can make the model "confess" but cannot prove the confession caused the act (Hewitt & Liang control tasks)
- **SAEs:** Placebo trial (Korznikov et al. 2026) — random baselines matched trained SAEs on interpretability, probing, and causal editing. Only 30% of features shared across seeds (Paulo & Belrose)
- **Activation oracles (incl. Anthropic's NLA):** Li et al. showed verbalizations can reflect the oracle model's own knowledge, not the target model's activations — ventriloquism, not revelation
- **AlphaGo parallel:** Move 37 (superhuman) and Move 78 (delusion) — same system can produce breakthroughs and bugs that look like breakthroughs
- **Next in series:** What it takes to build interpretability tools that earn the trust we already place in them

## Summary

Julius Adebayo delivers a devastating critique of interpretability research using a medieval priesthood metaphor. He traces how each generation of tools has repeated the same error — mistaking plausible-looking artifacts for faithful explanations — and marshals empirical evidence (weight randomization, control tasks, seed instability, placebo trials) to show the field hasn't learned. Directly challenges Anthropic's sparse autoencoder and Natural Language Autoencoder work. Marked `contested: true` in the LLM wiki.

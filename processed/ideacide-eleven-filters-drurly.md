# Ideacide — 11 filters (Dru Riley)

@DruRly framework for **killing ideas early** before multi-year commitment. Surviving examples in his post: HeadsUp, Multi, Charm.

## The 11 filters

| # | Filter | Pass criterion |
|---|--------|----------------|
| 1 | Personal use | You are first customer |
| 2 | Daily+ use | Compresses feedback; forces empathy |
| 3 | Platform potential | ≥1 of Helmer **Seven Powers** (scale, network, counter-positioning, switching costs, branding, cornered resource, process power) |
| 4 | Counter-positioning | Incumbent can't copy without hurting core revenue (not 50-me-too competitors) |
| 5 | Portfolio synergy | Strengthens other bets; avoids distraction (e.g. unrelated real estate) |
| 6 | Energy | Founder-nature fit — logic-yes / body-no is dangerous |
| 7 | Revenue proximity / urgency | Painkiller or near money; not perpetual nice-to-have |
| 8 | Low marginal cost | Software-like scaling vs linear fulfillment |
| 9 | Market size | ≥10k businesses **or** ≥10M people |
| 10 | Passive value | Delivers in background without customer effort |
| 11 | Evidence of demand | Search, communities, reviews, Reddit, competitor traction — or strong filter-1 signal |

## Why it matters in the vault

Pairs **product discipline** with agent-era noise: many “build X with Claude” ideas fail filters 3–4 and 11. Complements structured discovery ([[testing-business-ideas-product-operating-system]]) and harness focus ([[two-tracks-not-ten-agents]]).

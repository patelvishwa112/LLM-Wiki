# how to be good at research

## The Core Argument

Research isn't taught — it's reverse-engineered from visible outputs (papers, threads), producing people who look like researchers rather than being researchers. The actual skill is a stack of smaller, trainable skills.

## Eight Trainable Skills

1. **Pick your own problems** — Absorbed problems come without reasoning. Hamming: ask what the important problems are, then why you're not working on them. Schulman: choose an outcome you want to exist, reason backward. Taste is a muscle trained through prediction + correction cycles.

2. **Upgrade your inputs** — Shared reading lists produce shared ideas. Old material is underpriced (Mixture of Experts 1991, LSTMs 1997, Sutton's Bitter Lesson 2019, Shannon 1952). Read papers, not threads — the appendix and limitations section are the honest parts.

3. **Write everything down** — Writing exposes gaps your mind papers over. Feynman: don't fool yourself. Darwin: write down counter-evidence immediately. Keep a log: hypothesis → setup → expectation → result → updated belief. Publish publicly — it's an unfakeable credential.

4. **Tighten the loop** — Research speed = speed of discovering you're wrong. Radford: volume over genius. Karpathy: overfit a single batch before training at scale. Engineering and research have fused at the frontier.

5. **Stare at the outputs** — Loss curves are reassurance, not analysis. Ng: pull 100 failures, read them, sort into piles, attack the biggest. Most ML bugs live in data and fail silently.

6. **Wander on purpose** — First subfield is an accident. Spend time across interpretability, evals, RL, systems. Run disposable experiments. Tune baselines. Ablate — the winning component is usually not the one in the title.

7. **Find your people** — Open doors produce important work (Hamming). Generosity compounds: replicate, release tools, explain hard things. Float half-formed ideas. A collaborator who kills a bad idea early is worth more than compute.

8. **The long game** — Knowledge and productivity compound like interest. Daily edges look trivial; years later they look like luck.
